FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Why is there so much interest in fish oil and cardiovascular health?

Omega-3 fatty acids are essential — your body cannot produce them on its own. A substantial body of international research has explored the relationship between EPA, DHA, and cardiovascular function, and the American Heart Association (AHA) recommends adequate weekly Omega-3 intake. However, most modern diets fall short on Omega-3, especially for people who don't regularly eat deep-sea fish. Each Homega capsule provides EPA 312mg and DHA 156mg — a high-concentration nutritional supplement. We recommend pairing supplementation with a balanced diet and regular health check-ups.

Consult a healthcare professional if you have specific concerns.

What does IFOS 5-star certification mean?

IFOS (International Fish Oil Standards) is the world's most rigorous quality assessment program for fish oil. A 5-star rating — the highest possible — means the product meets or exceeds international benchmarks for purity, potency, and freshness. Testing covers heavy metals (mercury, lead), carcinogens (dioxins, PCBs), and oxidation levels. For context, Hong Kong's Consumer Council found contaminants in every single fish oil product they tested in 2022 — with carcinogens in over half. IFOS 5-star certification means Homega has been independently verified by a third party, not just the manufacturer.

When should I take Homega and how much?

Take Homega after a meal for best absorption. As a fat-soluble supplement, it absorbs more effectively when consumed with food. For active cardiovascular support (managing cholesterol, blood pressure, or circulation concerns), take 2 capsules per day. For general daily maintenance, 1 capsule per day is sufficient. Each box contains 60 capsules — that is approximately a 30-day supply at 2 per day, or a 60-day supply at 1 per day.

How is Homega different from pharmacy fish oil brands?

Three key differences. First, concentration: Homega delivers EPA 312mg and DHA 156mg per capsule. A typical pharmacy brand offers roughly EPA 180mg and DHA 120mg — Homega provides over 60% more combined EPA+DHA. Second, formulation: Homega uniquely includes Vitamin K2 (100mcg) and D3 (500IU), creating a triple-action formula for cardiovascular support that standard fish oil does not offer. Third, quality assurance: Homega holds the highest IFOS 5-star rating, is sourced from POLARIS in France, and undergoes molecular distillation to remove mercury, dioxins, and heavy metals. Most pharmacy brands do not provide independent third-party test results.

Are there any side effects of taking fish oil?

Side effects are generally mild. Some people experience a slight fishy aftertaste or occasional digestive discomfort — taking Homega with a meal usually minimizes this. You should consult your doctor before taking Homega if you are on blood-thinning medication (especially warfarin), about to undergo surgery, or pregnant/nursing. People taking warfarin should not take Homega, as fish oil has natural anticoagulant properties. If you experience bruising, bleeding gums, or an allergic reaction, stop taking Homega immediately and seek medical advice.

How long should I take Homega?

Nutritional supplementation is an ongoing process. Your body needs time to absorb and respond to nutrients, and everyone starts from a different baseline with different dietary and lifestyle habits. Our users generally commit to at least 90 days, giving their body adequate time to absorb and adjust. We recommend regular health check-ups before and during supplementation so you can track your own health indicators objectively.

Nutritional supplement outcomes vary by individual and are influenced by diet, exercise, and lifestyle factors.

Why does Homega include Vitamin K2 and D3?

This is the core of Homega's formulation philosophy. Most fish oils only deliver Omega-3, which addresses cholesterol. But cardiovascular health is not just about cholesterol — arterial calcification is an equally important risk factor. Vitamin K2 (100mcg MenaquinGold per capsule) activates Matrix GLA Protein, directing calcium to your bones instead of letting it deposit on artery walls. This supports arterial flexibility. Vitamin D3 (500IU per capsule) enhances calcium absorption and supports immune regulation. Together, these three ingredients address both cholesterol buildup and arterial calcification — offering more comprehensive cardiovascular support than Omega-3 alone.

Is Homega suitable for pregnant women, elderly, or children?

Homega is formulated for adults aged 18 and above, and is particularly suited for those over 40 who are focused on cardiovascular health. Pregnant and nursing women should not take Homega. For anyone under 18, consult a pediatrician first. Elderly adults can generally take Homega at the standard dosage, but should consult their doctor if they are on blood thinners or other long-term medications. People with diabetes, high blood pressure, or high cholesterol can take Homega. However, anyone on warfarin should not take this product.
